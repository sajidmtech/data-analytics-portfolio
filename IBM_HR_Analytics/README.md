# IBM HR Analytics

## Overview
Analysis of 1,470 IBM employees across three business questions 
using a real world Kaggle dataset.

## Tools Used
- MySQL / DataGrip — data querying
- Excel — summary tables, COUNTIFS, AVERAGEIF, conditional formatting
- Power BI — multi-page dashboard

## Business Questions
1. Which departments have the highest performing employees?
2. Does overtime affect attrition rates?
3. Are high performers compensated fairly?

## Key Findings
- Overtime workers leave at 3x the rate of non-overtime workers (30.5% vs 10.4%)
- High performers earn $200 less per month than average performers
- High performers receive larger salary hikes (21.85% vs 14%) but started on lower base salaries
- R&D accounts for 65% of all high performers

- GROUP BY, AVG, COUNT
- JOIN and LEFT JOIN
- CASE WHEN with multiple conditions
- Subqueries
- ROUND and TIMESTAMPDIFF
- COUNTIFS and AVERAGEIF in Excel
- Conditional formatting in Excel
